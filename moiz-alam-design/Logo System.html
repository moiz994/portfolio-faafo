Brand · Logo System

The MA ligature

A serif monogram set in EB Garamond, weight 500, with the two letters drawn tight so the M's stem and the A's leg nearly meet. It carries the portfolio's editorial voice straight into the mark.

MA
Construction

EB Garamond · weight 500 · letter-spacing −0.14em · line-height 1. The negative tracking is the whole idea — it's what makes the pair read as one mark rather than two initials. Spacing is set in em so the overlap holds at every size.

Light & Dark

Charcoal on cream, or cream on charcoal. The mark is monochrome only — it takes the foreground color of whatever surface it sits on. Never an accent color.

MACharcoal · #2A2A2A on cream
MACream · #F9F5ED on charcoal
Sizing

Scales from a 32px favicon up to full display. Below ~18px the ligature tightens further is unnecessary — let the favicon breathe a touch.

Clear space
MA

Keep clear space of at least half the mark's height on every side. Nothing — type, rules, or image edges — should intrude into it.

Companion symbol

For spaces too small or too busy for the ligature, the reference asterisk stands in — the literary footnote mark, set in the same Garamond. Use it as a favicon alternate, a section divider, or a loading glyph.

*

Garamond asterisk · weight 400 · monochrome. A quiet, distinctive atom that belongs to the same family as the ligature.

Do & Don't
MA
✓ Do — keep it tight, serif, mono

Preserve the −0.14em overlap. Ink or bone only. Let it sit on flat paper or black.

MA
✕ Don't — track it out

Spacing the letters apart breaks the ligature and turns it back into two loose initials.

MA
✕ Don't — use the sans

DM Sans is for UI, not the mark. The logo is always the serif.

MA
✕ Don't — recolor or add effects

No accent colors, gradients, outlines, or shadows. The brand is shadow-free.
