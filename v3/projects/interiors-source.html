The Challenge

The interior design trade market — the channel between manufacturers, wholesalers, and professional designers — had no central platform. Trade professionals were navigating a fragmented landscape of supplier relationships, paper catalogues, and phone calls to find products. The opportunity was to build the marketplace that connected this ecosystem.

The challenge was speed. Arbisoft needed to validate whether this was a real market worth building for, define the MVP, and produce a buildable spec — in weeks, not months. Traditional discovery methods wouldn't fit the timeline.

Approach

I ran an AI-first discovery process: using LLMs to rapidly synthesize market research, competitive landscape analysis, and persona development in parallel — work that traditionally required sequential research sprints. This wasn't AI replacing discovery; it was AI compressing the time between hypothesis and evidence.

Within the first week, I had a validated market map, a clear two-sided marketplace model (suppliers vs. trade professionals), and a prioritised list of core use cases. Week two went into translating that into a concrete MVP scope and feature spec.

The result was a discovery process that normally takes 4+ weeks delivered in two weeks — without cutting corners on the depth of understanding.

Outcome

Interiors Source moved from concept to buildable MVP spec in 2 weeks. The compressed discovery gave the engineering team a clear foundation to start building immediately, with no ambiguity about scope or priorities.

The project is a case study in how AI-assisted discovery changes the economics of early-stage product work — making it viable to move faster without sacrificing quality of thinking.
