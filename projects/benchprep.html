The Challenge

Hiring teams struggle to evaluate candidates consistently at scale. Structured interviews take time, require trained interviewers, and still produce subjective results. The question Arbisoft wanted to answer: could an AI system generate high-quality, role-specific interview questions and score responses reliably enough to be trusted by hiring teams?

The challenge wasn't just technical. It was also about defining what "reliable" meant — what level of AI evaluation quality would earn trust from recruiters, and what the minimum viable product needed to look like to test that assumption without over-building.

Approach

I defined the MVP scope with a tight constraint: prove that AI-generated questions and scoring could hold up in a real interview context before adding any workflow features. This meant choosing the right LLM evaluation criteria early, and building a feedback loop between the AI output and interviewer judgment.

Prompt engineering became a core product discipline — not a one-time setup. I ran 3 structured iteration cycles on the scoring prompts, each informed by structured feedback from pilot interviewers. Every iteration had a clear hypothesis, a defined test, and a specific outcome threshold.

On the delivery side, I coordinated the engineering team in two-week sprints, keeping scope contained so we didn't chase features before the core reliability was proven.

Outcome

BenchPrep shipped to beta in 14 weeks — a full AI-native interview pipeline covering question generation, candidate response capture, and LLM-based scoring with interviewer override.

Beta feedback confirmed that the scoring reliability was high enough for hiring teams to trust as a first-pass filter, which was the key validation gate we needed. The product is now in active use with pilot clients and moving toward general availability.
